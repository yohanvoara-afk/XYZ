Piece Details

Detailed Piece Information

King

Value: Infinite (game ends if captured)

Movement: One square in any direction

Special Move: Castling with rook

Starting Position: e1 (White), e8 (Black)

Important: The king must be protected at all costs!
Queen

Value: 9 points

Movement: Any number of squares horizontally, vertically, or diagonally

Power: Combines rook and bishop movements

Starting Position: d1 (White), d8 (Black)

Tip: Develop your queen early but don't expose it to danger!
Rook

Value: 5 points

Movement: Any number of squares horizontally or vertically

Special Move: Participates in castling

Starting Position: a1, h1 (White), a8, h8 (Black)

Strategy: Rooks are powerful on open files!
Bishop

Value: 3 points

Movement: Any number of squares diagonally

Color: Stays on same color squares entire game

Starting Position: c1, f1 (White), c8, f8 (Black)

Fact: Two bishops together are very powerful!
Knight

Value: 3 points

Movement: L-shape: 2 squares in one direction, 1 square perpendicular

Special: Only piece that can jump over other pieces

Starting Position: b1, g1 (White), b8, g8 (Black)

Unique: Knights are excellent in closed positions!
Pawn

Value: 1 point

Movement: Forward one square (two on first move)

Capture: Diagonally forward one square

Special: Can be promoted to any piece when reaching the opposite end

Power: "Pawn structure determines the outcome of the game"

Understanding Piece Values:

  • A rook is generally worth more than a bishop + knight
  • Two bishops are often worth more than two knights
  • Pawns become more valuable as they advance toward promotion
